## Research Task 3: Publisher & Platform Analysis

### Tier 1: Traditional Technical Publishers

#### 1. Pragmatic Bookshelf ⭐ **BEST FIT**

**Why Perfect Match**:
- Founded by Andy Hunt & Dave Thomas (Agile Manifesto co-authors)
- Focus: "Books by developers for developers"
- History: "The Pragmatic Programmer" became best-selling tech book
- Philosophy: Agile publishing business (breaks traditional molds)
- DRM-free, pioneered "beta book" concept

**Financial Model**:
- 50% profit split (vs 7-15% revenue at other publishers)
- Devil in details: Profit = Revenue - Publisher costs (editors, design, printing, marketing)
- End result similar to revenue models (~$15K-$20K for modest success)
- Best-case: $100K+ for breakout titles (rare)

**Author Experience**:
- Strict editorial process, high quality standards
- Dedicated development editors
- Strong reputation in tech community
- Distribution through O'Reilly network

**Submission**:
- Website: https://pragprog.com
- Executive Editor: Susannah Davidson
- Process: Author pitch through website

**Strategic Fit**: BMAD Method is AI-era evolution of agile - perfect alignment with Pragmatic's heritage

---

#### 2. O'Reilly Media

**Reputation**:
- #1 tech book publisher
- Highest credibility in industry
- Animal covers (distinctive brand)

**Financial**:
- 7-15% royalties on revenue
- Best-case: $100-200K lifetime for successful titles (Martin Kleppmann's "Designing Data Intensive Applications")
- Ebook + Safari Online often > print revenue

**Author Experience**:
- Most involved editorial process
- Highest bar for acceptance
- Strong marketing and distribution
- "Don't find us, we'll find you" reputation

**Submission**:
- Website: https://www.oreilly.com/authors

### Tier 2: Self-Publishing Platforms

#### 1. Leanpub ⭐ **RECOMMENDED FOR MVP**

**Why Recommended**:
- Write-in-progress model (publish chapters as you write)
- Built-in reader feedback loop
- Market validation BEFORE pitching traditional publishers
- Prove demand with sales data

**Financial**:
- 80% royalties to author (vs 7-15% traditional)
- Author sets pricing
- No upfront costs
- Example revenue: 500 copies × $39.99 = $19,995 gross, ~$16,000 author net

**Process**:
- Markdown-based workflow (perfect for technical books)
- Self-service: Create account, start writing
- Launch with "In Progress" label
- Early adopter pricing ($19.99 → $39.99 at completion)

**Strategic Value**:
- Build email list and audience
- Gather testimonials for traditional publisher pitch
- Validate market interest (is there demand?)
- Retain rights (can pitch to publishers later)

**Example**: Gergely Orosz self-published "The Software Engineer's Guidebook"
- $611,911 in royalties
- ~40,000 copies sold
- Started after leaving Manning

---

#### 2. Gumroad

**Model**:
- Direct sales platform
- 90% royalties (highest)
- Full pricing control
- No publishing services (just payment processing)

**Use Case**: Better for established authors with existing audience

## Research Task 4: Book Outline Structure Analysis

### Winning Pattern: Three-Part Structure

#### Why It Works

**Part 1: Theory (30%)** - Build mental models
- Problem statement resonates with reader pain
- Core principles provide framework
- Terminology creates shared language
- Philosophy explains "why" before "how"

**Part 2: Method (40%)** - Show detailed process
- Step-by-step workflows
- Decision rubrics for judgment calls
- Real examples from practice
- Roles and responsibilities clear

**Part 3: Application (30%)** - Prove it works
- Complete case study (OFAC project)
- Challenges and solutions
- Lessons learned
- Templates readers can use

**Part 1: THE BMAD METHOD - Foundations** (Chapters 1-6)
